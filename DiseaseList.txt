Diarrhea,  frequent, loose, or watery bowel movements caused by infections, food intolerances, or medications. Symptoms include cramps, urgency, and dehydration. Treatment focuses on rehydration and addressing the underlying cause.
Malaria, a mosquito-borne disease causing fever, chills, and flu-like symptoms. It requires prompt treatment to prevent severe complications.
Common cold, an infection causing nasal congestion, sore throat, and cough. Symptoms typically resolve within a week with rest and symptomatic relief.
TB, a bacterial infection primarily affecting the lungs, causing cough, fever, and weight loss. It can be serious if left untreated.
Pneumonia, an infection causing inflammation in the lungs, leading to symptoms like cough, fever, and difficulty breathing. It requires medical attention for treatment.
Influenza, a viral respiratory infection causing fever, body aches, and fatigue. It spreads easily and can lead to complications in vulnerable populations.
Cellulitis, a bacterial skin infection causing redness, swelling, and tenderness. It can spread rapidly and may require antibiotics for treatment.
Chicken Pox, a highly contagious viral infection characterized by itchy red spots or blisters. It typically occurs in childhood and can cause discomfort but usually resolves on its own without serious complications.
Migraine, a neurological condition causing severe headaches, often accompanied by nausea, sensitivity to light, and sound. It can significantly impair daily functioning.
Food Poisoning, illness from contaminated food causing symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.
Asthma,  a chronic respiratory condition causing breathing difficulties, wheezing, and coughing, often triggered by allergies or environmental factors.
